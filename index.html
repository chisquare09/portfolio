SRNC: Semi-supervised learning for robust novel cell-type identification in single cell RNA sequencing data. BMC Genomics. DOI: 10.1186/s12859-026-06386-3

  • Abstract:

    Classifying known and novel cell types in single-cell RNA sequencing (scRNA-seq) remains challenging. Supervised methods fail on unseen cell types, and rejection-based approaches underuse unlabeled data. We introduce SRNC (Semi-supervised learning for Robust Novel Cell-type identification), which integrates self-supervised feature learning with semi-supervised classification to leverage both labeled and unlabeled data. Evaluated on six benchmark scRNA-seq datasets, SRNC outperforms state-of-the-art methods, including MARS, in ARI, F1-score, and precision. It robustly identifies rare and novel cell populations, generalizes across datasets, and handles class imbalances effectively. SRNC provides a powerful, adaptable tool for discovering new cell types, advancing precision medicine, and enhancing understanding of cellular heterogeneity.

genCell: a diffusion-based generative
model for conditional single-cell RNA sequencing data
generation. The Rencontres de Quy Nhon VIII: International Biology Conference 2025

  • Abstract:

    The increasing demand for high-quality, cost-effective single-cell RNA sequencing (scRNA-seq) data has prompted interest in generative AI models that can simulate high-fidelity synthetic cells to support data augmentation, biomedical research, and biological discovery. Recently, diffusion-based models have emerged as a powerful alternative to traditional AI architectures and have demonstrated superior performance in many domains such as image synthesis or large language models, due to their stability and sample quality. However, the application for biological data, especially omics data, for instance scRNA-seq data remains underexplored. In this study, we propose genCell, a diffusion-based generative model to generate high-resolution  scRNA-seq data with biological conditions. We then investigate how different prior assumptions impact the training dynamics and generative capabilities of diffusion models tailored for single-cell data. Furthermore we adopt genCell to generate scRNA-seq data of lung fibrosis disease. Evaluation is conducted using various statistical metrics and nonstatistical metrics, which quantify the similarity between real and synthetic data. Our findings demonstrate that genCell could generate scRNA-seq data closely identical to real gene expression data; moreover, the choice of prior can significantly influence the model behavior and the quality of generated data. These experiments confirm that genCell is a powerful tool for conditional generation real scRNA-seq data, contributes to a deeper understanding of how probabilistics assumptions in generative modeling affect downstream biological interpretations and offers a high potential for serious disease treatment discovery in single-cell level.

Understanding Lung Fibrosis Progression through Conditional Generation of Single-cell Transcirptomes. USTH Student Scientific Research Competition 2025

  • Abstract:

    The growing availability of single-cell RNA sequencing (scRNA-seq) data has increased interest in generative models for synthetic cell simulation, aiding data augmentation, benchmarking, and biological discovery. Diffusion-based generative models offer advantages over traditional architectures like VAEs and GANs, but their application to single-cell data remains underexplored. Using benchmark datasets, including the Human Lung Cell Atlas (HLCA), we evaluate conditional data generation by Diffusion models and investigate how prior distribution assumptions affect generation quality. Our results show that Diffusion models outperform VAEs and GANs in conditional generation, and that prior choice significantly impacts model behavior and output quality. This work advances understanding of probabilistic assumptions in generative modeling and provides guidance for designing future single-cell simulation models.
